Status & roadmap.

Phases one through three are built and running on devnet: auth, auto-sweep with best-rate routing, and cold-forward with a hash-chained audit log. The splitter and a TOTP layer are designed but not yet shipped. The line to mainnet is a custody signer and an independent audit.

02 / UPCOMING

Designed, not shipped.

Planned

Atomic payout splitter

/send paying multiple recipients in one transaction, resolving aliases from a fixed contacts file or a literal address. Optional Groq parsing for looser phrasing.

Planned

Login + single-use TOTP

/login with an Argon2 password and a session, plus a burn-on-use TOTP required fresh for every fund-moving command. Today access is the allowlist only.

Planned

Group secret scrub

Auto-delete of any password or OTP typed into a group by mistake, and immediate burn of the code. Pairs with the DM-only fund protocol.

03 / BEFORE MAINNET

What has to land first.

Custody

Real KMS / HSM signer

The local keystore is devnet-only by design. Mainnet requires a custody-backed signer wired into the existing Signer interface — until then the bot refuses to start.

Limits

Withdrawal caps & rate limits

Per-window value ceilings and command rate limiting to bound the blast radius of a hijacked live session.

Resilience

Persisted OTP-burn & nonce state

Once the TOTP layer ships, replay and nonce tracking must survive restarts, so a process bounce can't reopen a used code.

Assurance

Independent third-party audit

Internal review and a test pass are done. An external audit is the last gate before real funds.

Items on this list are targets, not commitments with dates. This is a custody-boundary experiment — mainnet ships when it's earned, not scheduled.
